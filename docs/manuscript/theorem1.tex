\begin{theorem}[Circuit Uniqueness modulo Kernel]
Let $A$ be a binary-matroid representation over $\mathbb{F}_2$.
Let $S,T$ be support-minimal circuits of size $k+1$.
If
\[
\sum_{i\in S} A_{i,\cdot} = \sum_{i\in T} A_{i,\cdot},
\]
then $S$ and $T$ are equivalent up to coordinate permutation.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[Reduction target]
The search layer shows that raw support uniqueness and operator-value uniqueness both fail in small finite instances, so the correct invariant must be taken modulo the row-dependency kernel.
The remaining proof obligation is to derive permutation equivalence from equality of row-sums in the minimal support class.
\end{proof}
